Winter Weather Advisories Declared For East Idaho

By Jeff Papworth

The first of two forecasted snowstorms entered East Idaho on Wednesday morning and is continuing to bring precipitation to the region during one of the biggest travel weeks of the year, according to the National Weather Service.

After the current storm exits the area Thursday morning, another storm will bring widespread snow Friday. Authorities are urging the public to use extreme caution while driving for the Thanksgiving holiday. Winter storm warnings calling for a foot or more of snow from the first storm were declared for much of East Idaho, including the Arco, Spencer, Dubois, Island Park, Preston, Malad, Rockland, Malta, Holbrook, Thatcher and Montpelier areas.

The rest of East Idaho including the Pocatello, American Falls, Aberdeen, Burley, Blackfoot, Fort Hall, Idaho Falls, St. Anthony, Ashton, Driggs, Victor, Palisades, Soda Springs, Lava Hot Springs, Rigby and Rexburg areas are under winter weather advisories.The weather service is reporting that the central Idaho mountains are getting hit the hardest.
